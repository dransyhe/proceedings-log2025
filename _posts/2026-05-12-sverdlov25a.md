abstract: |
 Famously, the ability of Message Passing Neural Networks (MPNN) to distinguish between
 graphs is limited to graphs separable by the Weisfeiler-Lemann (WL) graph isomorphism
 test, and the strongest MPNNs, in terms of separation power, are WL-equivalent. However,
 it was demonstrated that the quality of separation provided by standard WL-equivalent
 MPNN can be very low, resulting in WL-separable graphs being mapped to very similar,
 hardly distinguishable outputs. This phenomenon can be explained by the recent
 observation that standard MPNNs are not lower-Lipschitz. This paper addresses this issue
 by introducing FSW-GNN, the first MPNN that is fully bi-Lipschitz with respect to
 standard WL-equivalent graph metrics. Empirically, we show that our MPNN is competitive
 with standard MPNNs for several graph learning tasks and is far more accurate in
 long-range tasks, due to its ability to avoid oversmoothing and oversquashing. Our code
 is available at https://github.com/yonatansverdlov/Over-squashing.
