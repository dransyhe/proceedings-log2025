abstract: |
 We propose a set of loss functions adapted from Stochastic Block Model (SBM) likelihood
 functions to train Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) for the task of unsupervised community
 detection. Identifying latent community structures is a prominent challenge for many
 graph applications. SBMs are classical models that describe the generating process of
 random graphs and are commonly used to infer community structure. The likelihood
 functions associated with SBMs are well-defined, differentiable, and measure the quality
 of inferred community partitions; this makes them particularly useful for unsupervised
 learning with GNNs. Our proposed loss functions are independent of any specific GNN
 architecture and demonstrate competitive or improved community detection performance
 against several alternatives. Evaluation is carried out on multiple architectures and
 datasets, offering a thorough empirical analysis of the state of community detection
 with GNNs.
